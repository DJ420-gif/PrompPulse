What Critique and Council Modes Do

Microsoft's March 2026 upgrade to 365 Copilot Researcher introduces two distinct multi-model orchestration approaches. Critique mode uses a two-stage pipeline: GPT-5.4 drafts an initial response, then Claude Sonnet 4.6 reviews that draft for accuracy, completeness and citation integrity before the response is returned to the user. The practical effect is a quality gate on every Researcher output — Claude's strong tendency to acknowledge uncertainty and flag unsupported claims functions as a fact-checking layer on GPT's initial generation. Council mode extends this further, coordinating multiple model perspectives on complex research questions to surface reasoning disagreements before settling on a final response. The DRACO benchmark result of 13.8% improvement in deep research accuracy is the most concrete independent validation available. DRACO tests multi-hop research tasks — questions requiring synthesis across multiple sources rather than direct retrieval — which is precisely where the Critique mode's accuracy review adds the most value.

02

Why Microsoft Is Using Both GPT and Claude Together

The decision to combine OpenAI's GPT-5.4 with Anthropic's Claude Sonnet 4.6 in a single Microsoft product reveals something important about the state of frontier AI: no single model dominates every task. GPT-5.4 has demonstrated strong generation breadth and speed. Claude Sonnet 4.6 has demonstrated strong accuracy and self-correction behaviour — the constitutional AI training that makes Claude more likely to flag when it is uncertain rather than confidently hallucinating. Microsoft's engineering insight is that these properties are complementary: use GPT for generation velocity, use Claude as an accuracy filter. This is not unique to Microsoft — enterprise AI teams have quietly been running similar multi-model architectures for months. What is new is Microsoft embedding this in a product at scale rather than requiring enterprise customers to build the orchestration themselves.

03

What the DRACO Benchmark Actually Tests

DRACO is Microsoft's deep research and complex operations benchmark — designed specifically to evaluate AI performance on the multi-hop research tasks that knowledge workers perform in real work contexts. The benchmark includes tasks like: synthesising information from multiple contradictory sources into a coherent summary, following complex multi-step research instructions across different document types, and maintaining factual accuracy when integrating information from diverse references. A 13.8% improvement on DRACO is significant because the tasks it measures are exactly what Copilot Researcher is used for in production — not simple question answering that any capable model handles well, but the complex research synthesis tasks where model errors create real professional risk for the human reviewing the output.

04

Should You Use Copilot Researcher for Professional Research

The Critique and Council upgrade makes Copilot Researcher a meaningfully more reliable tool for professional research tasks that require citation accuracy and source synthesis. The practical recommendation is: use it for first-draft research synthesis where human review is the final gate, not as the sole research source for consequential decisions. The 13.8% DRACO improvement is real but a 13.8% improvement over a previous baseline still leaves room for errors in production. The multi-model architecture reduces hallucination frequency — it does not eliminate it. For regulated industries where research accuracy has legal or financial consequences, the Compliance API plus the Critique mode together create a more defensible AI research workflow than either alone. The Compliance API provides the audit trail. The Critique mode provides the accuracy review layer. Neither is a guarantee — both reduce risk.

Critique mode uses GPT-5.4 to draft responses and Claude Sonnet 4.6 to review them for accuracy, completeness and citation integrity before returning output to the user. Scores 13.8% higher on the DRACO deep research benchmark versus single-model approach.
Why does Microsoft Copilot use both GPT and Claude?
GPT-5.4 provides strong generation breadth and speed. Claude Sonnet 4.6 provides accuracy review and uncertainty flagging — complementary capabilities that produce higher research accuracy when combined than either model delivers alone.
What is the DRACO benchmark?
Microsoft's deep research and complex operations benchmark — tests multi-hop research tasks requiring synthesis across multiple sources, following complex multi-step research instructions and maintaining factual accuracy with diverse references. Designed to reflect real professional research workflows.
Is Copilot Researcher multi-model available now?
Yes — Critique and Council modes are available for Microsoft 365 Copilot subscribers as of the March 2026 upgrade. Access through the Microsoft 365 Copilot Researcher agent within Teams, SharePoint and the Copilot interface.
Copilot Researcher vs Perplexity for research — which is better?
Perplexity is better for ad-hoc web research with real-time citations. Copilot Researcher is better for synthesis tasks across your organisation's internal documents and knowledge bases. The Critique mode's accuracy improvement makes Copilot more reliable for complex multi-source synthesis tasks that Perplexity is not designed for.
