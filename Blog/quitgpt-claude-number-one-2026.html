What Happened on February 27

February 27 2026 produced the most dramatic 24-hour sequence in AI consumer history. The morning: OpenAI CEO Sam Altman publicly stated he shared Anthropic's position on restricting military AI uses — specifically Anthropic's refusal to allow Claude for mass surveillance or autonomous weapons. Hours later that same day: OpenAI signed a classified Pentagon deployment contract. Anthropic had refused the same deal. President Trump responded by posting on Truth Social ordering all federal agencies to immediately cease using Anthropic products. The Defense Department's Hegseth designated Anthropic a supply chain risk. By evening, consumer backlash against OpenAI was spreading across Reddit, X and Instagram. A Reddit thread calling for ChatGPT cancellations crossed 33,000 upvotes in hours.

The QuitGPT Wave — Real Numbers

The QuitGPT and Cancel ChatGPT campaigns launched February 28 attracted over 1.5 million participants within 48 hours. ChatGPT uninstalls surged 295% overnight. The signal that mattered most: Claude's daily downloads briefly overtook ChatGPT's in the US App Store — not in total users, but in daily downloads, the metric that shows where new user intent is flowing. Claude reached number one on the US Apple App Store on March 1. Anthropic confirmed a 60% increase in free active users and quadrupled daily signups. Paid subscriptions more than doubled. Anthropic made memory import from ChatGPT free for all users on March 2 — explicitly reducing switching friction during the surge.

Trump Bans Anthropic — The Paradox

The Trump administration's response created an unintended dynamic. An executive order directed all federal agencies to cease using Anthropic products.
